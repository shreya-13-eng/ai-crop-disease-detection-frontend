Detection Results

Tomato Late Blight
Confidence:
94%
Severity:
Medium

Late blight is a serious fungal disease that affects tomatoes and potatoes. It spreads rapidly in cool, wet conditions and can destroy entire crops if not treated promptly.

Important: This is an AI-based diagnosis. For critical infections, consult with local agricultural experts for confirmation.

Recommended Treatments

Chemical Fungicide Application

Apply copper-based fungicide (e.g., copper oxychloride) at 2g per liter of water. Spray every 7-10 days until conditions dry.

Apply immediately, reapply after rain
Organic Solution Baking Soda Spray

Mix 1 tablespoon baking soda, 1 teaspoon vegetable oil, and a few drops of dish soap in 1 liter of water. Spray on affected plants.

Apply every 5-7 days
Prevention Cultural Practices
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants for air circulation
  • Water at the base, avoid wetting leaves
  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts
  • Rotate crops next season

Tips for Best Results

  • Take photo in good natural light
  • Focus on affected leaves/stems/fruits
  • Include both healthy and diseased parts if possible
  • Take multiple photos from different angles
  • Avoid blurry or shadowed images
